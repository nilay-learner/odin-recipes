Chokha-Bati

Description

Chokha Baati is a traditional Rajasthani dish featuring chokha, a smoky mashed vegetable mix of eggplant, potatoes, and tomatoes, and baati, a hard, round bread made from whole wheat flour and ghee. Together, they create a hearty, flavorful meal that showcases Rajasthan's rich culinary heritage.

Ingredients

For chokha
  • Eggplant (brinjal)
  • Potatoes
  • Tomatoes
  • Garlic
  • Green chilies
  • Fresh coriander
  • Mustard oil
  • Salt
  • Spices (cumin, turmeric, red chili powder)
For bati
  • Whole wheat flour
  • Ghee (clarified butter)
  • Salt
  • Water (for kneading)
Additional
  • ghee (for serving)
  • Spices (like ajwain or black pepper) for flavoring baati

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven or prepare a clay oven for baking baati.
  2. For baati, mix whole wheat flour, ghee, and salt in a bowl.
  3. Add water gradually and knead the dough until smooth.
  4. Divide the dough into small balls and set aside.
  5. For chokha, roast the eggplant over an open flame until the skin is charred and the flesh is soft.
  6. Boil the potatoes and mash them in a bowl.
  7. Once the eggplant is cool, peel off the skin and mash it with the potatoes and tomatoes.
  8. Add chopped garlic, green chilies, fresh coriander, mustard oil, salt, and spices to the mashed mixture.
  9. Mix well and adjust seasoning as needed.
  10. Place the baati balls in the preheated oven and bake until golden brown and crisp.
  11. Serve hot baati with melted ghee and chokha on the side.
